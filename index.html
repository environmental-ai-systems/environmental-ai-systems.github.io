Research Group

Environmental Modelling, Data & AI Systems Group

We are a dynamic research group within the Department of Earth and Environmental Sciences at the University of Manchester, bringing together early-career researchers through to senior faculty. Our collaborative environment welcomes researchers at all career stages who share our passion for environmental modelling, data science, and AI/LLM-enabled systems — with a strong emphasis on teaching, training, and reproducible research.

Remit & Themes

We bring together staff and students working across:

  • Environmental modelling (process-based, statistical, hybrid) Building and applying models to understand environmental systems, from physics-based simulations to data-driven approaches
  • Data science & analytics (methods, workflows, reproducibility) Developing robust analytical pipelines and methods for processing, analyzing, and visualizing environmental data
  • AI & LLM-enabled systems (tools for analysis, synthesis, decision support) Leveraging large language models and AI tools to accelerate research, synthesize information, and support decision-making
  • Data/AI systems (pipelines, evaluation, infrastructure, open resources) Building the technical infrastructure and evaluation frameworks needed for reliable data and AI workflows
  • Teaching & training (courses, short workshops, skills development) Sharing knowledge and building capacity through courses, workshops, and hands-on training programs

Our research focuses on:

  • Models & inference: calibration, uncertainty, emulation, scenarios Improving how we fit models to data, quantify uncertainty, and use models for prediction and scenario analysis
  • Data & methods: QA/QC, integration, spatiotemporal methods Ensuring data quality, combining diverse data sources, and developing methods for spatial and temporal analysis
  • AI systems: LLM tooling, retrieval, evaluation, human-in-the-loop workflows Creating practical AI systems that combine language models with domain expertise and human oversight
  • Open research: reusable code, transparent methods, collaborative practice Promoting transparency and reproducibility through open-source code, clear documentation, and collaborative approaches

PhD Opportunities

  • Propose a PhD project - Information on developing your own research ideas or applying to advertised projects We welcome applications from prospective PhD students. You can either: - Apply to advertised projects - Propose your own research ideas aligned with our expertise Common funding schemes include: - NERC Doctoral Training Partnerships (DTP) - EPSRC Centres for Doctoral Training (CDT) - ESRC PhD studentships - University of Manchester President's Doctoral Scholarships Application deadlines typically fall between January and March. Browse our departmental funding pages, or speak to individual faculty members, to find available scholarships. Contact us 6-12 months before your intended start date.

Visiting Researchers

  • Academic visitors program - Spend time collaborating with our research group Visitor facilities include: • Desk space and computer access • Library and computing resources • Seminar participation • Research collaboration opportunities Typical visit duration: 1 week to 12 months. Most visitors arrange their own funding, but limited support may be available. Contact us at least 3 months in advance.
  • Sabbatical opportunities - Information for faculty on sabbatical visits We welcome visiting faculty on sabbatical: • Typical duration: 3-12 months • Desk space and research facilities provided • Opportunities to co-supervise students • Collaborative research projects • Guest lecturing by arrangement Limited accommodation support may be available. Contact us 6-12 months in advance to plan your visit.
  • Short-term research visits - 1-6 month research exchanges Short-term visits are available for: • PhD students (research exchanges) • Early-career researchers • Masters students on research placements Typical activities: • Collaborative research • Skills training and workshops • Data analysis and modeling work • Research seminars Visitors typically need their own funding (e.g., travel grants, exchange programs).
